# research-enrichment
Every week, we will each select a research paper and build a presentation on it as if we were the authors of our chosen papers.
* Megan Kwok
* Amelia Kwok

## goals
* gain familiarity with the CS/AI research landscape
* build muscle in digesting research papers
* practice presenting to peers
* practice coming up with questions on the paper
  * for now, we'll start with pre-reading others' research papers before presentations
  * want to expand to including practice on coming up with questions on the spot (thoughts on if this is necessary?)
* being able to defend research and predict any questions that may be asked on it
  * expectation is that we will have done some further research on the topics adjacent to the main concerns in the paper

## organization
* Mondays: paper selection due
* Saturdays: presentation (put link in) + questions/comments/concerns (Q/C/C) from audience
* presentation can be in person/zoom/hybrid depending on who's where
* feel free to share notes in the repo
  
## notes on paper selection
* scholar.google.com to start
* you can get use chatbots to get keywords for your topic/narrow it down
* ensure it is not a literature review
  * abstract/title mentions it being any sort of review (systematic, analytical, narrative, etc) of research done in the field
  * lit reviews are analyses of recent, relevant research in the field and identify knowledge gaps, but **do not contribute new research** 
  * review of tools is passable, but also not fully aligned with the goals of this project
  * lit reviews are good for finding other people's research: go to bibliography and find the actual research being referenced and look through those papers
* try to select a paper less than 20 pages for your own sanity (feel free though, just have to explain all of it)
* to submit selection: create new row, enter the paper title and authors in papers.md under "Paper" and "Authors" and push

# notes on presentation
* option 1: make a google slideshow and link it papers.md under "Presentation" column
* option 2: make a markdown in the repo (preferred for easy access, you can also add images in mds too) and put the filename in papers.md (ex: 06-16-26-present.md) and scroll when presenting
* don't put paragraphs --> use bullet points, you can have pages of notes on the side
* keep it to 10-15 minutes
